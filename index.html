Education

Clean Surroundings Tips
Learn & Act

01

Garbage Segregation

Separate waste at home into dry (plastic, paper) and wet (food, organic) to help proper disposal and recycling.

Download PDF Guide
02

Avoid Open Dumping

Use covered bins and avoid throwing garbage in streets or drains. Open dumping spreads diseases and attracts pests.

Download Awareness PDF
03

Control Stagnant Water

Water collected in pots, tires, or trash becomes a breeding ground for mosquitoes. Empty it every 2–3 days.

Download Water Control PDF
How-To

Process to Keep
Surroundings Clean

1
Sweep around the house and common areas daily or at least 3 times a week.
2
Segregate waste: Use green bins for wet waste and blue bins for dry waste.
3
Avoid dumping garbage in open areas. Use community bins if available.
4
Cover and regularly clean drains to prevent mosquito breeding.
5
Empty stagnant water from vessels, tires, or pots every 2–3 days.
6
Encourage neighbors to participate in maintaining street cleanliness.
Solutions

Common Issues &
Practical Solutions

Lack of toilets at home
Use community toilets or apply under the Swachh Bharat Yojana.
Open garbage dumping
Segregate and use dustbins; talk to local panchayat to place bins nearby.
Stagnant water leads to mosquitoes
Drain water every 2 days and cover open pots.
No awareness about government health schemes
Educate people about Aarogya Sri, Anganwadi services, and vaccinations.

Environment

Waste Management
Awareness

♻️ Segregate waste into dry and wet categories at the source.
🗑️ Use color-coded bins: Green for biodegradable, Blue for recyclables, Red for hazardous.
🌱 Compost kitchen waste like vegetable peels, tea leaves, and leftovers.
🚫 Avoid burning waste as it causes air pollution and health issues.
🤝 Participate in local waste drives and encourage neighbors to join.
📢 Report illegal dumping or overflowing bins to local authorities or panchayat.

Say No to Plastic
Use These Alternatives

Bags Cloth, jute, or paper bags instead of plastic carry bags
🍶
Bottles Metal or glass water bottles instead of plastic
🥄

Conservation

Water Conservation
for a Clean Environment

🚿
Avoid water overflow while filling tanks, buckets, or public taps to prevent puddles and mosquito breeding.
🔧
Fix public and home water leaks by reporting them to local authorities or using plumbing services.
🌾
Promote drip irrigation in gardens, farms, and village lands to avoid excess water usage.
🌧️
Implement rainwater harvesting in homes, schools, and public buildings to recharge groundwater.
🚫
Do not dump waste or chemicals into ponds, lakes, or streams—protect community water bodies.
🧹
Clean drainage paths regularly to allow rainwater to flow and prevent flooding and contamination.
💧
Use collected rainwater or RO waste for cleaning streets or watering trees in the neighborhood.

About

About This
Project

"Clean Habits, Healthy Surroundings" is a Community Service Project undertaken by students to promote health awareness through digital means. The project covers topics like personal hygiene, water safety, sanitation, and surroundings cleanliness. We aim to motivate people to adopt small changes that result in a big impact on health.
