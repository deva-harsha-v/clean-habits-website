Clean Habits, Healthy Surroundings

A Community Service Project on Health & Sanitation Awareness

🏠 Introduction

This web application is a digital initiative developed as part of our CSP project to raise awareness about health, hygiene, and cleanliness in rural and semi-urban areas. It is designed to educate users, collect their responses, and share solutions for daily sanitation and personal hygiene challenges. The app serves as a digital alternative to physical surveys, helping us reach wider audiences.

🧹 Clean Surroundings Tips – Learn & Act

1. Garbage Segregation

Separate waste at home into dry (plastic, paper) and wet (food, organic) to help proper disposal and recycling.

📥 Download PDF Guide

2. Avoid Open Dumping

Use covered bins and avoid throwing garbage in streets or drains. Open dumping spreads diseases and attracts pests.

📥 Download Awareness PDF

3. Control Stagnant Water

Water collected in pots, tires, or trash becomes a breeding ground for mosquitoes. Empty it every 2–3 days.

📥 Download Water Control PDF

🧹 Process to Keep Surroundings Clean

  1. Sweep around the house and common areas daily or at least 3 times a week.
  2. Segregate waste: Use green bins for wet waste and blue bins for dry waste.
  3. Avoid dumping garbage in open areas. Use community bins if available.
  4. Cover and regularly clean drains to prevent mosquito breeding.
  5. Empty stagnant water from vessels, tires, or pots every 2–3 days.
  6. Encourage neighbors to participate in maintaining street cleanliness.

🚫 Common Issues & Practical Solutions

  • Issue: Lack of toilets at home
    Solution: Use community toilets or apply under the Swachh Bharat Yojana.
  • Issue: Open garbage dumping
    Solution: Segregate and use dustbins; talk to local panchayat to place bins nearby.
  • Issue: Stagnant water leads to mosquitoes
    Solution: Drain water every 2 days and cover open pots.
  • Issue: No awareness about government health schemes
    Solution: Educate people about Aarogya Sri, Anganwadi services, and vaccinations.

♻️ Waste Management Awareness

  • Segregate waste into dry and wet categories at the source.
  • Use color-coded bins: Green for biodegradable, Blue for recyclables, Red for hazardous.
  • Compost kitchen waste like vegetable peels, tea leaves, and leftovers.
  • Avoid burning waste as it causes air pollution and health issues.
  • Participate in local waste drives and encourage neighbors to join.
  • Report illegal dumping or overflowing bins to local authorities or panchayat.

🛍️ Say No to Plastic: Use These Alternatives

  • Use cloth bags, jute bags, or paper bags instead of plastic carry bags.
  • Switch to metal or glass water bottles instead of plastic bottles.
  • Use wooden or steel cutlery for events rather than plastic spoons/forks.
  • Buy groceries in bulk to reduce plastic packaging.
  • Try beeswax wraps or steel lunch boxes instead of cling film and plastic containers.
  • Support local stores that promote zero-waste shopping.

💧 Water Conservation for a Clean Environment

  • 🚿 Avoid water overflow while filling tanks, buckets, or public taps to prevent puddles and mosquito breeding.
  • 🔧 Fix public and home water leaks by reporting them to local authorities or using plumbing services.
  • 🌾 Promote drip irrigation in gardens, farms, and village lands to avoid excess water usage.
  • 🌧️ Implement rainwater harvesting in homes, schools, and public buildings to recharge groundwater.
  • 🚫 Do not dump waste or chemicals into ponds, lakes, or streams—protect community water bodies.
  • 🧹 Clean drainage paths regularly to allow rainwater to flow and prevent flooding and contamination.
  • 💧 Use collected rainwater or RO waste for cleaning streets or watering trees in the neighborhood.
